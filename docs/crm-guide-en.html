›Before you start

The essentials to run the system day to day.

This is the system we use to manage pickups, deliveries, storage, trips and money — the full life of every moving job. The key things:

  • Everything is live: if a teammate adds or changes something, you see it instantly — no need to reload the page.
  • It runs in the browser (computer or phone). Nothing to install.
  • Your work screen is Dispatching. That's where you'll spend 80% of your time.
  • What you see depends on your permissions. If a section is missing from your menu, you don't have access to it (ask an admin).
  • The app can be displayed in English or Spanish — switch it in Settings. This guide shows the English labels.

4Jobs — create, edit, and the job timeline

Tap "+ New job", top right in Dispatching, Jobs or the Calendar.

Full

Pickup → storage → delivery. It's stored in between.

Direct

Pickup → delivery. Straight through, no storage.

Broker delivery

Delivery only: the goods are already here, you just deliver them.

The form adapts to the type — it only shows the fields that apply.

What you fill in

  • Where it's stored — tick one or more rented units and/or warehouses (a job can occupy several places).
  • Job data — Job #, Customer (+ phone and email), Type, Status, Broker, Rep, Driver (one or several), FADD, Volume (CF), Lot #, Sticker color, Date in.
  • Pickup — date (or a date range), address, city, state, zip and the balance to collect at pickup.
  • Delivery — date, address, city, state, zip and the balance to collect at delivery.
  • Carrier / BOL (broker deliveries only) — rate per CF, BOL balance, pads received/returned.
  • Storage billing — whether the client pays monthly for storage (see section 7).

✏️ Edit later, inline

Tap the Job # to open the detail and click almost any field to change it on the spot (FADD, customer, driver, addresses, balances…). There's also an Edit button for the full form. Payments recorded in the job form flow straight into the Payments module.

📏 Real CF vs. estimate

The broker sends an estimated volume; when the crew measures the real load, enter the Real CF on the job. Storage and truck occupancy calculations use the real figure once it exists.

The job timeline (events)

Besides the main status, each job keeps a timeline of events you can log as things happen — they build the job's history and can suggest the matching status:

Picked up Loaded to truck Dropped at storage Loaded from storage Delivered On hold Attempted delivery — not home Redispatched Driver handoff Other

7Billing — charging storage to the client

Some clients pay a monthly fee to store their goods. The system handles the cycle automatically.

Turn it on in the job

In the job form, Storage billing section: tick "Charge this client for storage", enter the monthly rate, choose whether the 1st month is free (the start date is computed automatically, but editable).

The Billing page

Metrics (Total pending, Overdue, Due this week, Collected this month) and tabs (All / Pending / Overdue / Paid). The system auto-generates each 30-day cycle's charge and flips past-due items to Overdue on its own.

Collect

Mark as paid records the date and amount.

Remind

💬 Reminder opens WhatsApp with a payment reminder ready to send to the client (it deliberately never mentions where the goods are stored).

8Brokers

The companies we do jobs for.

List of brokers (Allied, Atlas, Mayflower, United, North American, Wheaton, Arpin, Bekins… plus "Direct — no broker") with contact, phone and email. For each one you see how many jobs they have and the total pending balance. You can add, edit or delete brokers; when creating a job you pick the broker from this list.

9Settlements — broker closing sheets

For broker deliveries: each closing sheet groups a broker's jobs and keeps score of the money.

  • Metrics + tabs: Open / Settled / Disputed / All, plus totals like "Broker owes us", "We owe brokers", outstanding BOL and pads outstanding.
  • Each sheet shows the jobs included, CF, carrier fee, BOL collected, pads, costs and the net settlement (whether the broker owes you or you owe them).
  • In the detail you record the BOL collection (amount, method, date). That collection syncs automatically with Payments — money is never entered twice.
  • Jobs are linked to a closing sheet from the job detail — no double data entry.
  • Button to export the closing sheet to PDF (and a WhatsApp summary to send it).

10Extras & Commissions

Charging the extras of each job and splitting the commission between drivers and reps.

There are 7 extra types: Extra CF, Shuttle, Long carry, Stairs, Packing, Flight charge and Other. The section has two tabs — 🧑‍✈️ Drivers (grouped by driver) and 👤 Reps / Back office (grouped by rep) — with monthly metrics on top: total extras, driver commission, rep commission and what's left for the company.

Adding an extra

  • In the Drivers view every job lists the 7 extra types as rows — tick the box to activate one and fill in the amount, the driver (pre-filled from the job), generated by (Driver only, Driver + Rep or Rep only), the rep if one was involved, and the commission % (pre-filled by the rules below, editable).
  • You can also add extras from the job detail → Extras section → "+ Add extra".

11Trips / Live Load — trucks on the road

A trip puts several jobs on one truck and tracks the load and the stops live.

Creating a trip

  • "+ Trip" → automatic number (TRIP-001…), pick the truck, the driver, the departure date, then search and add jobs. A capacity bar shows how much CF is left as you add. Reorder stops with ↑ / ↓ or by dragging.
  • You can also add custom stops that aren't jobs (fuel, hotel, warehouse visit…) — they merge into the same ordered stop sequence, have a category, and can be edited by clicking them.
  • 🤖 AI trip suggestions: the system can propose which jobs to combine on a trip, considering each job's real load point (storage vs. pickup) and explaining the reasoning per trip.

Active trips

  • One card per active truck: truck + driver, capacity bar (green <70%, amber 70-90%, red >90%), and the ordered list of stops — each with job, customer, route, CF, sticker, FADD and balance to collect (pending extras included). Totals of CF and money at the bottom.
  • Per stop: Mark delivered, or Dropped at storage — the job stays on the trip faded out, marked "Dropped in storage", and you pick the unit/warehouse per job (you can even create a new unit right there).
  • Route — a popup with the full journey; its stops can be re-ordered and ticked off as completed, and the state is saved per trip.
  • ✂️ Split — divide a job between two trucks/trips (part of the load goes on each); you pick the destination trip/truck.
  • 🔁 Driver handoff — hand jobs from one driver to another mid-trip, with a reason; the handoff is logged in the history.
  • 💬 Send manifest to the driver — WhatsApp with the trip's full stop list; there are also mid-trip update messages.
  • Depart marks the trip in transit; when every job is delivered the trip completes automatically (any drops already made are respected, and stored jobs get their storage set per job).
  • Relocations: internal moves between storages (no charge to the client) are supported as their own trip purpose.
  • 🧰 Equipment tab: track equipment & materials (pads, dollies…) loaded/unloaded per truck and trip.
Live map: the Trips section includes a live truck map of the US showing where each active trip is along its route.

Payments: month metrics, the red "deposit this!" alert, and the tabs.

Recording a payment ("+ Payment")

  • Job (search by number or customer — there's also a payment search by job number), date, amount, concept (Job / Extra / CC Fee / Storage / Other) and method (cash, check, Zelle, Venmo…).
  • Checks / money orders ask for the number/ID and type (personal / cashier / business). Optional discount + reason. Payment stage: At pickup / At delivery / Other.
  • Received by whom and when. For cash, checks and money orders: who holds the money (pre-filled with the job's driver) and whether it's been deposited (date + bank account). Digital methods (Zelle, Venmo…) are marked deposited automatically.
  • Allocation per charge: a payment is applied against the job's charges (pickup balance, delivery balance, extras…) and the system shows the remaining amount per charge — so partial payments always add up correctly and extras already invoiced aren't double-counted.

👛 "In circulation" view

Groups the money by the person holding it: total on hand, breakdown (cash / checks / money orders) and the per-job detail. 💬 Request deposit opens WhatsApp asking them to deposit. From here you can also run a batch deposit — tick several payments and deposit them together into a bank account (bank accounts are managed in this module too).

📈 Weekly cash flow

At the bottom: expected, received, in circulation and deposited for the week, plus a projection of the deposited total if everything in circulation gets deposited.

Auto-sync: recording a BOL collection in Settlements creates/updates the payment in Payments, and vice-versa. Payments can also be added from the job detail (pre-filled with the outstanding balance and the job's driver). Money is never entered twice.

13Drivers & Trucks

🪪 Drivers

Drivers appear automatically as you assign them to jobs. Tap one to open their profile: how much money they hold in circulation (cash / checks / money orders not yet deposited), their payment history (what they received and when they deposited it) and their job history.

🚛 Trucks

The fleet: name, plate and capacity in CF. Each truck shows its current live occupancy when it's out on a trip.

14BOL & Documents — paperwork and signatures

The BOL module generates, annotates and gets the Bill of Lading signed — on the spot or remotely.

  • Generate the BOL over the PDF template: configurable fields, checkboxes and free text stamped onto the document — click on the PDF to place each value where it belongs. Edited values cover the old ones cleanly.
  • Sign at pickup: the customer signs on screen. A new version created after a signed pickup inherits the signature and goes straight to the delivery step.
  • Sign at delivery: signed on the document itself, with the pickup signature still visible.
  • Remote signing: send the BOL by email for the customer to sign (the back-office flow), or through the DocuSign integration (pickup + delivery) — the system gets notified when the envelope is completed.
  • Documents: all generated paperwork is filed in folders per broker.
  • AI assist: the system can read/parse BOL documents with AI to help fill in data.

17Clients, Analytics & Suggestions

18Users, roles & settings

Who can see and do what.

  • Two roles: Admin (full access, including the Users section) and Member.
  • Members get per-section permissions with three levels: View, Edit, Create. Sections without View simply don't appear in that user's menu. (Suggestions is open to everyone; Users is admin-only.)
  • Admins invite users by email with a permissions grid, can activate/deactivate accounts, trigger password resets and see each user's last login.
  • In Settings every user can change their own password and the display language (English / Spanish).

19Deleting a job 🗑

You can delete a job from the job detail (red "Delete job" button), from the Jobs list, or from Dispatching. It always asks for confirmation. Deleting removes the job and cleans up everything related — extras, payments, and its link to the closing sheet.

⚠️ It cannot be undone. If in doubt, use Cancelled or On hold instead of deleting.

20Golden rules & glossary

🎯 The 6 rules of the day

1. Check Dispatching and tackle the red/orange FADD first.
2. Make sure no pickup/delivery for today is left without a driver, and chase the 🟥 Not collected tags.
3. Always notify the driver with the 💬 WhatsApp button (it already includes all the data, or the trip manifest).
4. Advance the status as soon as each stage happens, so the board reflects reality.
5. Check the red alert in Payments (cash undeposited >7 days) and the In circulation view.
6. Anything broken, missing or complained about → open a Claim right away.
