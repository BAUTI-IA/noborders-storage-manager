1The money map — read this first

One page that explains the whole system. Everything else is detail.

The CRM tracks money in two parallel worlds, and one module that ties them together:

🧾 The operational world — what should happen

Jobs create charges (balances, extras, BOL, storage billing). Payments records every dollar collected against those charges, who physically holds it, and when it reached the bank. Expenses records every dollar spent (fuel, hotels, payroll, materials). These are our books.

🏛️ The bank world — what actually happened

Banks is the real bank ledger: every movement imported from the bank statements, categorized by one person and verified by a second one, then rolled into the official P&L. The bank never lies — this is the ground truth.

🔗 Reconciliation is the bridge. The Banks → Conciliación tab cross-checks both worlds: every deposit recorded in Payments should appear in the bank, every bank-paid expense should have its bank line, and every bank movement should have an operational explanation. When all three lists are empty, "the numbers add up ✓". Anything left over is exactly where to look for missing records — or missing money.

💵 The life of one dollar (worked example)

1A charge is born. Job #2214 is created: $800 due at pickup, $1,200 at delivery. On the road the client adds packing → an extra of $200 is registered. Total expected: $2,200.
2It gets collected. The driver collects $800 cash at pickup. Someone enters it in Payments (+ Payment): method cash, received by the driver — the system marks who has the money. It is now in circulation: received but not in the bank.
3It gets allocated. At delivery the client pays $1,500 by Zelle. Using "Assign to charges" the payment is split: $1,200 to the delivery balance, $200 to the packing extra, $100 left on account. Zelle is digital → marked deposited automatically.
4It reaches the bank. The driver deposits the $800 cash; in the In circulation view it's ticked and batch-deposited to "Chase Operations". Payments now shows: received $2,300 · in circulation $0 · deposited $2,300.
5The bank confirms it. The statement is imported into Banks (screenshot or CSV). The $800 deposit and the $1,500 Zelle appear, get categorized ("Job"), and a second person verifies them. Reconciliation matches them against Payments — no discrepancies.
6It becomes profit. The verified inflows land in the P&L as Revenue; the driver's pay for the day (Expenses → Días trabajados), his commission on the extra, and the fuel he bought flow in as costs. Revenue − costs = what the company actually made on the job.

3Payments — collections, circulation, deposits 💰

The heart of finance: every dollar received, who physically holds it, and whether it reached the bank.

The three states of a collected dollar

Pending
charge exists, not paid→ Received / In circulation
someone holds it physically→ Deposited (banked)
it's in the bank account
💳 The golden rule of methods. Digital payments (Zelle, Venmo, credit card, wire, PayPal, Cash App, Apple Pay) go straight to deposited — the money lands in an account by itself. Physical payments (cash, checks, money orders) stay in circulation — a person is literally carrying that money — until someone marks them deposited. Circulation is where money gets lost; that's why the module watches it so hard.

Entering a payment (+ Payment)

  • Job (search by number or client) · date · amount · concept: Job / Extra / CC Fee / Storage / On account / Other · optional discount + reason (net = amount − discount) · stage (at pickup / at delivery / other).
  • Method-specific fields:
    • Check — type (cashier's / personal / official), serial number with live duplicate warning (catches the same check entered twice), bank/issuer, date, memo, and a photo of the check.
    • Money order — type (USPS / Western Union / MoneyGram / other), serial (also dup-checked), office, purchaser, and a photo.
    • Credit card — tick "Charge CC fee to client" (3% default): the system computes the total to collect and creates the separate CC Fee payment automatically.
  • Received by (person + date). For physical methods: "Who has the money?" — auto-filled with the job's driver — and, if already deposited, the date and bank account.

Allocation — "Assign to charges" ✂️ (the most important habit)

A client rarely pays charge by charge — they hand over one amount. Allocation is how one payment gets split across the job's charges so every charge shows its true remaining balance:

  • Tick "✂️ Assign to charges (split)" in the payment modal. One line appears per charge: the job balance and each pending extra, each showing "Pending: $X".
  • Type an amount per line — the system proposes a sensible split automatically (job balance first, then extras oldest-first) and shows the remainder per charge live. Overpaying a line is allowed (flagged), but the lines can never exceed the payment total.
  • Anything you don't assign stays "On account" — received but not applied. It shows on the job as "A cuenta (sin imputar)" with an Assign button; use it later to apply that money to charges (the modal reopens with the amount locked and the split pre-spread over what's still owed).
  • + New extra / another line creates a brand-new charge on the spot (choosing the concept — extra CF, shuttle, long carry…) and it immediately enters the commission system.
Why it matters: without allocation, a job can look "paid" in total while a $200 extra is actually unpaid (and its commission unpayable), or look unpaid while money sits on account. Allocation keeps every charge's remaining balance true — which is what the Dispatching labels, the trip "To collect", and the commissions all read.

In circulation — the anti-loss view

  • The In circulation tab groups undeposited money by the person holding it: total on hand, breakdown (cash / checks / money orders), and each payment with the days held.
  • 💬 Request deposit — one click opens WhatsApp with the list of what they're holding and a request to deposit by end of week.
  • Batch deposit — tick items (or Select all), pick the bank account and date, hit ✓ Deposit N · $total: all of them are marked banked in one shot, with the account recorded (that's what reconciliation will match against the statement).
  • The red alert on top of Payments lists everything received but not deposited for 7+ days — that list should be empty. Chase it daily.

Reading the numbers

  • Metrics: Expected this month · Received (green) · In circulation (red — all undeposited money regardless of month) · Deposited (blue) · Pending collection (orange) · CC fees (purple). Note: In circulation ≠ Received − Deposited, because it includes older months' cash still out there.
  • Weekly cash flow (bottom): expected / received / in circulation / deposited for the current week, plus the projection if everything in circulation were deposited.
  • 🔎 Job verdict: search a job number and a banner answers "does this job have payments?" — ✅ with count/total/received, 🚫 "No payments recorded", or ⚠️ no such job.
🔄 One entry, never two. Recording a BOL collection in Settlements (or on the job's Carrier Settlement section) automatically creates/updates the matching payment in Payments — and vice versa. Money is entered once, wherever it's most convenient.

4Extras & Commissions ➕

Extras are additional charges on a job; a slice goes to whoever generated them.

  • The 7 types: Extra CF · Shuttle · Long carry · Stairs · Packing · Flight charge · Other.
  • Registering one: from the job detail (+ Add extra), from the Extras page (each job shows the 7 types as tickable rows), or automatically when a payment split creates a new extra line.
  • Each extra records: amount, the driver who generated it (defaults to the job's driver), "generated by" (Driver only / Driver + Rep / Rep only), the rep if involved, and the commission percentages — auto-filled by the rules below, editable per case.
Extra typeDriver onlyDriver + RepRep only
Extra CF / Packing / Flight / OtherDriver 10%Driver 7% / Rep 3%Rep 10%
Long carry / StairsAlways driver 50% — the company keeps the other 50%
ShuttleDriver 10%Driver 7% / Rep 3%Rep 5%
  • The Extras page has two tabs — 🧑‍✈️ Drivers and 👤 Reps / Back office — each grouping extras by person, with month metrics on top: total extras, driver commission (green), rep commission (blue), and what's left for the company (amber).
  • Under each person: a TOTAL row and a yellow COMMISSION row (Excel-style), with 📋 Copy and 🖨️ PDF buttons to send each driver/rep their commission summary.
  • Each rep has a profile (tap the name): commission per month and full extra history.
⚠️ An extra only pays commission when it's collected. Keep extras allocated (section 3) — an unpaid extra shows as pending on the job and in the driver's summary, and unallocated "on account" money doesn't count toward it.

5Settlements — broker closing sheets 📑

For broker deliveries: the running account between us and each broker.

  • A closing sheet groups a broker's jobs. For each job it tracks: CF delivered, the carrier fee (what the broker pays us to deliver — auto-computed), the BOL balance and what was collected at the door, pads, and costs.
  • The sheet's bottom line is the net settlement: collected BOL money is ours to keep against the carrier fee — the net says who owes whom (broker → us, or us → broker).
  • Workflow tabs: Open (still accumulating jobs) → Settled (paid out) → Disputed. Jobs are linked from the job detail (Carrier Settlement section), where you can also move a job to another open sheet or a new one.
  • Record payment on a job's BOL (amount, method, date) — synced automatically with Payments (never entered twice). The collection status badge shows Collected / Partial / Pending.
  • Export to PDF — the closing sheet formatted for sending to the broker.
Watch for: the orange Collection pending label on Dispatching — a broker delivery already delivered whose BOL isn't fully collected. That's money at the door that didn't come back.

6Storage Billing — recurring monthly revenue 🧾

Clients who keep goods in storage pay a monthly fee. The system runs the cycle by itself.

  • Setup (once per job): in the job form, tick "Bill this client for storage", set the monthly rate, and choose whether the first month is free — the start date computes itself from the date the goods came in.
  • Every 30 days the system generates the charge automatically and marks it overdue when unpaid past the due date. No one has to remember.
  • The Billing page: metrics (total pending, overdue, due this week, collected this month) and tabs (All / Pending / Overdue / Paid). Per charge: Mark paid and a 💬 Reminder — a pre-written WhatsApp to the client.
  • Overdue storage billing also appears in the Dispatching metrics bar (Billing overdue, red) so it's never out of sight.

7Expenses — spend, driver cash & the weekly payroll 💸

The outflow side: what the operation spends, and what each driver earns and owes.

7.1 Expenses with approval (Gastos tab)

  • + Expense: date, category (Fuel ⛽ / Hotel 🏨 / Materials 📦 / Tolls 🛣️ / Maintenance 🔧 / Meals 🍔 / Other), amount, vendor, optional driver / truck / trip / job #, and — always — the receipt photo or PDF. Fuel expenses add gallons, odometer and IFTA state, with $/gallon computed.
  • Paid with is the key field: Bank account (pick which — reconciliation will look for it on the statement), Company card, Other, or Driver cash (from collections) — see below.
  • Approval flow: every expense starts Pending; someone with edit rights hits ✅ Approve or ❌ Reject. Only approved expenses count in P&L and Driver P&L math.
💵 Driver cash — the loop that must close. When a driver pays fuel or tolls out of the cash he collected from clients, record the expense as "paid with driver cash". The system keeps the reconciliation live: cash on hand − unsettled expenses = what he should hand in. When he hands in the remainder, hit 🤝 "settled" on those rows. The "Driver cash not settled" tile on top tells you how much collected money is currently being spent on the road instead of deposited — keep it near zero.

7.2 Días trabajados — the weekly pay grid 📆

  • The pay week runs Wednesday → Tuesday and is paid the following Wednesday. Navigate weeks with ← / → ; the blue pill shows the payout date.
  • One row per driver, one cell per day. Click a cell to cycle: empty → ✓ full day (pays the daily rate) → ½ half day (half the rate) → hourly (asks the hours; pays hours × hourly rate) → empty.
  • Rates live on the driver record (Daily rate $/day, Hourly rate $/hour). The rate is frozen the moment you mark the day — a later raise never rewrites weeks already recorded. A driver with no rate shows "no rate set" in red: fix that first.
  • + Adjustment (fuck-up / bonus): a deduction (client's mirror broken, no-show, missing material…) lowers that week's pay; a bonus raises it. Always entered as a positive amount + type + reason + optional job #. The week's adjustments are listed under the grid, and the driver's row shows Days · Pay · Adjustments · Week total.
  • The bottom row — "Total to pay on Wed DD/MM" — is the payroll number for the whole team. That payment then shows up in Banks as Salaries when it hits the statement.

7.3 Materials 📦

  • A catalog of supplies (pads, boxes, shrink wrap…) with unit costs, and a movement log: Purchase (into stock) / Issue (handed to a driver) / Return / Consume (used on a job) / Adjust.
  • The "On hand per driver" panel computes issued − returned − consumed: a red balance is material the driver still owes back, valued in dollars. Chase it like cash.
📊 Driver P&L — what each driver really earns you. Per driver and month: revenue attributed from his jobs − labor (work days × frozen rates) − approved expenses − commissions ± adjustments = net and margin. Visible in Analytics and in the driver's profile. This is why marking days, approving expenses and allocating extras matters — they're the inputs.

8Banks — real ledger, double check, P&L 🏛️

Everything above is what we say happened. Banks is what the bank says happened — and the bank wins.

8.1 The lifecycle of a bank movement

Imported
Sin revisar→ Categorized
by person A→ Verified ✔
by person B — never A→ Counted in P&L
👀 The double check is the anti-fraud control. The person who verifies a movement must be different from the person who categorized it — the system physically blocks self-verification. Editing an already-verified movement reopens the double check. No single person can quietly decide what a bank movement "was".

8.2 Getting movements in (⬆ Importar)

MethodHow it worksLands as
📸 Screenshot (AI)Upload homebanking screenshots; the AI extracts every line (date, description, amount, direction, suggested category with confidence) into a "Review before importing" table you confirm. The screenshot itself is stored as evidence.Sin revisar
📄 Bank CSVUpload the bank's CSV export, map the columns (date / description / amount, or debit + credit), extract, review, confirm. AI suggests categories in batch.Sin revisar
🗂️ CSV maestroThe bookkeeper's master export with an account_name column: rows route to each account automatically (names must match exactly), thousands at a time, with a preview of per-account counts and totals.Verified directly (already categorized)

No duplicates, ever: every import is deduplicated by account + date + amount + description — re-uploading the same statement is harmless. And the Bandeja organizes everything in 📁 month folders with a red badge for what's still unreviewed; inside, pages of 100 with server-side filters (status, account, direction, category, text search) so totals always reflect the whole ledger.

8.3 Accounts & categories

  • Cuentas: each bank account with opening balance and a computed balance (opening + all non-ignored movements). If the computed balance doesn't match homebanking, imports are missing — that's the tell.
  • Categorías: the chart of accounts — the same categories as the bookkeeper's Bank Flows Excel (Job, Refund, Hotels, Fuel, Salaries, Toll, Truck Repair, Storage, Fees, Taxes, Broker, Marketing, Transfer Between Accounts…), each with a direction and a P&L group (Cost of Revenues / Production / Structure / Sales & Marketing / Broker / CapEx). Renaming a category updates all its movements; deactivating hides it without touching history.

8.4 Conciliación — does it all add up?

  • Matches bank lines against Payments (deposits: amount + date within tolerance) and Expenses (bank-paid). Internal transfers are excluded.
  • Three discrepancy lists, each an action item:
    • Bank money with no backing — a movement with no operational record. Investigate: unrecorded income, an expense nobody logged… or worse.
    • Deposit that never hit the bank — a payment marked deposited that the statement doesn't show. Where is it?
    • Expense with no bank line — recorded as bank-paid but absent from the statement (or that period isn't imported yet).
  • Green summary — "the numbers add up ✓" — is the goal state every week.

8.5 The P&L — the company's real income statement

  • Classic layout, months as columns, expenses in red parentheses: Revenue → (Cost of Revenues) → GROSS PROFIT → (Production) → (Structure) → (Sales & Marketing) → (Broker) → (CapEx) → NET PROFIT, with margin % tiles on top.
  • "Verified only" is on by default — the P&L is built exclusively on double-checked data.
  • Excluded on purpose: transfers between accounts, and Financing / Financing-Loan / Financing-Capital / Credit Card Payment — they move cash but are not income or operating expense, so they'd distort profitability.

9Claims & Incidents — money at risk ⚠️

Every incident is a potential payout. Track it from report to resolution, with the dollars attached.

  • Report with + New claim (or from the job/trip detail, pre-filled): incident date, type (💥 Damage / 🚨 Theft / 🔍 Missing items / 📭 Incomplete delivery / 😠 Complaint / 📝 Other), assignee, description.
  • The two money fields: Claimed $ (what the client demands) vs Paid / settled $ (what it actually cost us), plus the resolution: paid to client / denied / covered by insurance / discount applied.
  • Workflow: Open → Investigating → Resolved (or Denied). Attach evidence (photos/PDFs) and keep the follow-up log — every status change is logged automatically. Active claims older than 14 days turn red.
  • Top metrics: open, investigating, resolved this year, total claimed vs total paid, average days to close. Claim payouts should reappear in Banks under the "Claims" category — another reconciliation point.
  • If a driver caused the damage, the corresponding deduction is entered in Expenses → + Adjustment, linked to the job — so the cost lands on his week and his P&L, not just the company's.

10The finance routines — daily, weekly, monthly ✅

☀️ Every day

  • Enter every collection in Payments the day it happens, allocated to its charges.
  • Chase the red alert: nothing 7+ days in circulation.
  • Check Dispatching for Not collected and Collection pending labels.
  • Log road expenses with receipts; approve or reject what's pending.

📆 Every week

  • Complete the work-days grid and adjustments; pay Wednesday's total.
  • Batch-deposit what's in circulation; settle driver cash 🤝.
  • Import the week's bank movements; categorize; get them verified by a second person.
  • Open Conciliación — drive the three lists to zero.

🗓️ Every month

  • Review Storage Billing overdue and send reminders.
  • Settle broker closing sheets; export PDFs.
  • Send drivers/reps their commission PDFs.
  • Read the P&L (verified only) and the Analytics alerts — negative-margin units, AR aging, vacancy bleed.
